Trust & Evidence

How we keep reviews honest

Two systems work together: computer vision that can't be fooled by edited images, and a citation layer that keeps ingredient claims accountable — even when they push back on mainstream science.

📷

AI Image Authenticity Detection

Every photo attached to a review — product shots, skin reactions, results — passes through a computer vision pipeline that checks for signs of AI generation and post-processing manipulation:

  • ✓ GAN/diffusion fingerprinting — detects artifacts left by image generators that are invisible to the human eye
  • ✓ Edit-region detection — flags localized patches of inpainting or clone-stamping used to hide skin reactions or alter product labels
  • ✓ Metadata & provenance checks — cross-references EXIF data, device fingerprints, and upload patterns against the claimed purchase date
  • ✓ Professional credential verification — doctors and dermatologists uploading certifications go through the same pipeline to prevent fake credentials
Images that fail are rejected silently — the submitter sees a generic upload error, not the specific reason, to prevent gaming the system.
🔬

Cited Claims — Including Dissenting Ones

Any claim about an ingredient — that it's safe, harmful, overhyped, or misunderstood — must be backed by a source. But "the mainstream literature" isn't the only valid source.

  • ✓ Peer-reviewed studies — DOI-linked papers from PubMed, Cochrane, or equivalent journals
  • ✓ Regulatory documents — EPA, FDA, EWG, IARC classifications and safety sheets
  • ✓ Preprints & emerging research — labeled clearly as preliminary, not yet peer-reviewed
  • ✓ Dissenting literature — studies that contradict consensus are shown, clearly labeled as minority positions

Why dissent matters

History is full of cases where the community was right before the science caught up — BPA, trans fats, certain preservatives. A sourced dissenting claim isn't misinformation; it's a signal worth tracking. We surface it transparently so readers can weigh the evidence themselves.

Questions

FAQ

Do I have to buy through Healthly to leave a review?

You can always leave a review without a verified purchase — but only verified buyers receive revenue sharing. Unverified reviews are still shown, just labeled accordingly.

When do I get paid?

Payouts process once the retailer's affiliate commission clears (typically 30–60 days after purchase, to account for returns). You'll see a pending balance in your account in the meantime.

What if I return the product?

If the retailer reverses the affiliate commission due to a return, the pending payout is also canceled. You keep any review you submitted — it'll just be unflagged as verified.

Does Healthly cost anything?

Nope. Scanning, searching, building a profile, and reading reviews are always free. We make money only when you choose to buy through us — and we share it back with you.

Are professional / expert reviews paid differently?

Yes — verified professionals (dermatologists, dietitians, etc.) who pass our AI certification check receive a higher revenue-share rate as a thanks for lending their expertise.
